I grin when I hear people brag that they became a leader after an "intensive" two week class. It takes months or years to develop leadership skills, like resilience, judgment, and EQ.
Selling shoes at my dad's shoe store in Kentucky, when I was 14 years old, really helped me develop an ownership mentality and focus on results. Look for weird, unique opportunities to develop your leadership skills.
Every time I mentor a young student, I learn just as much as he or she does...otherwise I consider the relationship a total failure.
Empathy is a key soft skill, I learn just as much as he or she does...otherwise I consider the relationship a total failure.
Although I always root against Duke basketball, I must admit, I loved spending time with Coach K and learning more about his leadership philosophy.
There's no such ting as a perfect leader. The trick is finding and fixing your blindspots and red flags, because others certainly will.
